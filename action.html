find out how you can do your part below!

here are some small changes you can make to your everyday life to live more sustainably! these changes are easy and inexpensive! you can also help by sharing these tips with your friends and family!

save your vegetable water!

at my house, my mom keeps a bucket in the sink where she dumps all the water from washing fruits and vegetables (no soap or oil). then she uses that water to water our plants or to rinse dishes before putting them in the dish washer. this way, you save water and money!



shop for clothes less often / secondhand!

fast fashion is a huge contributor to landfill waste. by shopping less, or making more conscious purchases, you can reduce textile waste! another reason not to support fast fashion is that their workers are often paid little and work in harsh conditions. it's totally okay if you do buy from fast fashion brands if you need to though, remember, that companies are the ones that need to change their habits! you as a consumer are just trying to discourage companies from implementing those harmful practices by not buying from them. shopping secondhand gives clothing a second life and prevents the clothing from going to the landfill!

eat less meat and dairy!

it takes a lot of energy and water to raise livestock, so eating less animal products means that less grain needs to be grown to feed the livestock and less water has to be used. growing the grain also takes water, energy and is sometimes related to deforestation (to clear land to grow the grain ). some alternatives to dairy are nut milks and other non-dairy products. for meat, some substitutions are beans and tofu, which are in many cases cheaper than meat!



don't use plastic straws if you don't need them!

of course, if you need to use plastic straws, go ahead! but know that there are other options, like paper, bamboo and metal straws! investing in a metal straw is a one time purchase, as long as you take good care of it. and sometimes, you just don't need a straw! normalize drinking out of cups without straws!



carpool, bike, or walk instead of driving!

carpooling, biking and walking are all ways to reduce your greenhouse gas emissions! doing this also saves money since you don't have to pay for as much gas! you also get more exercise!



bring a reusable grocery bag when shopping!

a reusable bag can hold more stuff and sometimes be stronger than a plastic bag! this way, you don't have to pay for the plastic bag at the store or put more plastic in the environment.



when it's cold, put on more layers instead of turning on the heater!

this saves money you would be spending on gas, and reduces the your greenhouse gas emissions!
